# Enterprise Exception Policy

## Purpose
This policy defines circumstances under which standard pricing guidelines may be adjusted for enterprise clients.  
Large organizations often operate websites with significantly greater scale and complexity than typical engagements.

Enterprise exceptions allow the agency to adapt pricing structures when standard packages do not accurately represent the required effort.

## Enterprise Characteristics
Enterprise clients typically demonstrate one or more of the following characteristics:

- websites containing thousands or millions of pages  
- multiple regional or international domains  
- complex internal systems or CMS architectures  
- dedicated in-house marketing and engineering teams  

These characteristics often increase the complexity of SEO work.

## Exception Conditions
Pricing exceptions may be considered when a project includes:

- large-scale site migrations  
- multi-domain SEO strategies  
- international or multilingual optimization  
- enterprise-level technical constraints  

In such cases the scope of analysis and coordination increases substantially.

## Diagnostic Complexity
Enterprise environments require deeper investigation before implementation work begins.

Diagnostic efforts may involve:

- large-scale crawl analysis  
- log file analysis  
- cross-domain canonical evaluation  
- coordination with engineering teams  

These activities require specialized expertise and additional time.

## Pricing Adjustment Approach
Instead of applying standard service packages, enterprise engagements may be structured using custom project scopes.

Pricing adjustments may reflect:

- extended diagnostic phases  
- cross-team coordination  
- additional reporting requirements  
- large-scale implementation planning  

Custom pricing ensures the engagement remains feasible for both parties.

## Proposal Structure
Enterprise proposals should clearly outline:

- diagnostic scope and methodology  
- implementation phases  
- stakeholder coordination requirements  
- expected project timelines  

Structured proposals help enterprise clients evaluate resource commitments.

## Approval Process
Enterprise pricing exceptions require internal review before final approval.

Sales teams should document:

- reasons for the exception  
- project complexity indicators  
- estimated resource allocation  

Management approval ensures pricing decisions remain consistent across engagements.

## Risk Considerations
Enterprise projects may involve higher operational risk.

Examples include:

- large infrastructure dependencies  
- deployment restrictions  
- cross-department coordination challenges  

Pricing adjustments should account for these operational complexities.

## Documentation
All enterprise exceptions must be documented within the proposal record.

Documentation should include:

- justification for exception pricing  
- final approved scope  
- internal approval notes  

Maintaining records ensures transparency and consistent future decision-making.
