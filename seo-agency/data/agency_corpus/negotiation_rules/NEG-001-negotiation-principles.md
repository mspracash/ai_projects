# Negotiation Principles

## Purpose
This document defines the general principles guiding price negotiations
between the agency and prospective clients. Negotiations should protect
the integrity of the agency’s services while maintaining flexibility
for legitimate budget constraints.

The goal is not simply to lower prices but to structure engagements
that still deliver meaningful outcomes for the client.

## Core Principle
Negotiations should always preserve the effectiveness of the work.
Reducing price by removing critical analysis or planning activities
often results in ineffective outcomes.

Instead of removing key services, the scope of execution should be
adjusted to fit the available budget.

## Value Communication
Sales teams should begin negotiations by explaining the value of the
recommended services. Clients often focus on price before fully
understanding the work involved.

Clear explanations of diagnostic work, planning steps, and execution
effort help clients appreciate why certain services are necessary.

Clients who understand the process are more likely to accept realistic
project scopes.

## Scope Adjustment Strategy
When clients cannot support the full recommended engagement, the first
option should be scope adjustment rather than price reduction.

Examples include:
reducing the number of pages optimized
limiting the number of keyword clusters analyzed
focusing on priority sections of the website

This approach keeps the strategic structure of the work intact.

## Phased Engagements
Many negotiations can be resolved by dividing the project into phases.

A typical phased structure includes:
initial diagnostic analysis
strategy development
implementation across prioritized pages

This approach allows clients to begin with a smaller investment while
maintaining the long-term roadmap.

## Pricing Integrity
Discounting should be applied carefully and consistently.
Frequent or excessive discounting reduces perceived service value
and creates unrealistic expectations for future engagements.

Sales teams should follow the agency’s discount policies when
considering price adjustments.

## Client Qualification
Negotiation discussions can also reveal whether a client is a good
fit for the agency’s services.

Clients unwilling to support essential investigative work may
have expectations that cannot be met within limited budgets.

Identifying such situations early protects both parties.

## Documentation
All negotiated agreements should be documented clearly.

The final proposal should specify:
services included
services excluded
project timeline
expected deliverables

Clear documentation prevents misunderstandings later.

## Warning Signals
Sales teams should be cautious when negotiations focus exclusively
on price reductions.

Clients who repeatedly request discounts without adjusting scope
may undervalue the complexity of SEO work.

These situations should be reviewed carefully before proceeding.

## Outcome Principle
Successful negotiations create agreements where both parties benefit.
The client receives meaningful strategic work and the agency maintains
the quality of its services.

Negotiations should therefore aim to balance flexibility with
professional standards.
