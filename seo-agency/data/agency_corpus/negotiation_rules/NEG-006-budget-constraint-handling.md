# Budget Constraint Handling

## Purpose
This rule explains how sales teams should respond when a client’s budget
cannot support the full recommended SEO engagement.

The goal is to align project scope with realistic financial constraints
while preserving the strategic effectiveness of the work.

Budget limitations are common during early discussions with prospective
clients. Proper handling ensures the engagement still produces meaningful
results.

## Guiding Principle
Budget constraints should primarily be addressed through **scope alignment**
rather than aggressive discounting.

Reducing price without adjusting the scope often leads to incomplete work
and unrealistic client expectations.

Maintaining a structured workflow ensures the engagement still produces
actionable insights.

## Diagnostic Preservation
Diagnostic activities should rarely be removed during budget negotiations.

Examples include:

- technical SEO audits
- keyword research
- site architecture review
- ranking diagnostics

These activities identify the root cause of the client's problem.
Without proper diagnosis, implementation work becomes ineffective.

## Scope Adjustment Strategy
When budgets are limited, the preferred approach is to reduce execution
volume rather than investigative work.

Examples include:

- optimizing a smaller number of pages
- focusing on priority keyword clusters
- limiting content updates to high-impact articles
- implementing improvements in phases

This preserves the strategic foundation of the engagement.

## Phased Engagement Approach
Budget constraints may also be addressed by dividing the project into phases.

Typical structure:

Phase 1: diagnostic analysis  
Phase 2: strategic planning  
Phase 3: targeted implementation  

Phased engagements allow clients to begin with a smaller investment while
maintaining a long-term improvement roadmap.

## Discount Considerations
Discounts should be used carefully and within agency policy guidelines.

When appropriate, sales teams may combine scope reduction with the
**New Client Onboarding Discount** described in NEG-005.

Discounts must always respect the limits defined in **PRC-003 Discount Cap Policy**.

## Client Communication
Clients should clearly understand why diagnostic work remains essential.

Explaining how investigation informs implementation helps clients
appreciate the value of the proposed services.

Clear communication often resolves budget concerns without major price reductions.

## Warning Signals
Sales teams should be cautious if a client:

- insists on removing diagnostic work entirely
- expects guaranteed results with minimal investment
- repeatedly renegotiates pricing without adjusting scope

These signals may indicate unrealistic expectations.

## Outcome Principle
Successful budget negotiations balance affordability with strategic
integrity.

A well-structured engagement should still provide the client with
actionable insights and measurable improvements.
