# Escalation and Approval Rules

## Purpose
This document defines when negotiation decisions must be escalated for
additional approval.

Certain pricing adjustments or scope changes may exceed the authority of
individual sales representatives.

## Escalation Triggers
Escalation should occur when:

discount requests exceed standard thresholds
clients request removal of diagnostic services
the engagement requires unusual scope adjustments

These cases require review by senior strategists or pricing managers.

## Approval Structure
Escalated cases should follow a clear review process.

The sales representative documents the request and provides context about
the client’s situation.

A pricing lead then evaluates whether the request can be approved.

## Evaluation Criteria
Approvals should consider several factors:

expected project complexity
long‑term value of the client relationship
impact on service quality

The agency should prioritize sustainable outcomes over short‑term revenue.

## Documentation Requirements
All escalated negotiations must be recorded in the project documentation.

Notes should include:

requested change
reason for the request
final approval decision

Maintaining these records improves consistency.

## Communication With Clients
When escalation occurs, clients should be informed that the request is under
review.

Clear communication helps maintain trust during the negotiation process.

## Exception Handling
Occasionally exceptions may be granted when unique circumstances justify them.

However, exceptions should remain rare and carefully documented.

## Risk Management
Escalation procedures protect the agency from accepting engagements that
cannot deliver meaningful results.

They also prevent inconsistent pricing decisions.

## Outcome
A structured escalation process ensures negotiation decisions remain aligned
with agency policies and service standards.
