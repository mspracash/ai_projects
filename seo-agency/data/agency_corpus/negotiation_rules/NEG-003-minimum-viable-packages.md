# Minimum Viable Packages

## Purpose
Minimum viable packages allow the agency to deliver meaningful value even
when a client has a limited budget.

These packages focus on the most critical diagnostic and remediation tasks.

## Core Concept
The objective of a minimum viable package is to identify the root cause of a
problem and provide a clear plan for improvement.

Even small engagements should generate useful strategic insights.

## Typical Components
Minimum packages often include:

technical site review
keyword opportunity analysis
basic indexing checks
initial recommendations

These components reveal the main issues affecting search performance.

## Budget‑Constrained Engagements
Clients sometimes approach the agency with urgent problems but limited funds.

In these situations, the minimum viable package allows the agency to begin
with focused diagnostic work.

Once the root cause is identified, the client may decide to expand the
engagement.

## Benefits
Minimum viable packages help clients understand the value of professional SEO
analysis before committing to a larger engagement.

They also prevent clients from implementing incorrect solutions based on
assumptions.

## Execution Model
The engagement typically follows three stages.

diagnostic analysis  
problem identification  
recommended action plan  

Implementation may occur later depending on client resources.

## Client Education
Sales teams should explain that the package provides insight rather than a
complete solution.

Understanding the problem is the first step toward resolving it.

## Use Cases
Minimum viable packages are particularly useful for:

small businesses with limited budgets
organizations investigating a sudden ranking decline
companies evaluating SEO providers

## Outcome Expectations
Although these engagements are smaller, they should still produce clear
recommendations supported by evidence.
