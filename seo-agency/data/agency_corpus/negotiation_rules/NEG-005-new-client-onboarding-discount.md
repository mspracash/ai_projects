# New Client Onboarding Discount

## Purpose
This rule defines a standardized onboarding discount that may be offered
to first-time clients. The goal is to lower the initial barrier to engagement
while preserving the long-term pricing integrity of the agency’s services.

## Discount Rule
New clients may receive a **10 percent onboarding discount** on their
first engagement with the agency.

The discount must follow these conditions:

- maximum discount cap of **$2,000**
- applicable to **first engagement only**
- not reusable for future projects

This ensures consistency in how onboarding incentives are applied.

## Eligibility
The onboarding discount may be offered when:

- the client has **not previously engaged the agency**
- the project represents a **first diagnostic or exploratory engagement**
- the client demonstrates **budget sensitivity during initial discussions**

The discount should always be positioned as a one-time onboarding incentive.

## Scope Integrity
The onboarding discount should **not remove or weaken core diagnostic work**.

Essential investigative services such as:

- technical SEO audits
- keyword research
- diagnostic analysis

must remain intact to ensure the project produces meaningful insights.

## Client Communication
Sales teams should clearly communicate that:

- the discount applies only to the **initial engagement**
- future engagements follow **standard pricing policies**
- the onboarding incentive exists to **start the relationship**

Setting expectations early prevents pricing misunderstandings later.

## Approval Guidelines
Sales representatives may apply the onboarding discount within the
defined limits.

Discounts exceeding the **$2,000 cap** require management approval.

All applied discounts should be recorded within the proposal documentation.

## Warning Signals
Sales teams should be cautious if:

- clients attempt to extend the onboarding discount to later projects
- negotiations focus exclusively on price reduction
- the client repeatedly requests additional discounts

These signals may indicate unrealistic pricing expectations.

## Outcome Principle
The onboarding discount is designed to initiate new client relationships
without undermining the value of agency services.

A successful first engagement often leads to longer-term partnerships
and additional strategic work.
