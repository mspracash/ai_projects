# Scope Reduction Rules

## Purpose
This document explains how service scope may be reduced when a client’s budget
cannot support a full engagement. The goal is to maintain service effectiveness
while adapting the scope to realistic financial constraints.

Scope reduction should always preserve the logical order of SEO work.
Investigation and planning steps must remain intact whenever possible.

## Negotiation Position
When a client requests a lower price, the sales team should first evaluate
whether the requested budget can support meaningful results.

Instead of removing essential work, the engagement may be divided into phases.
The first phase may include investigation and planning, followed by
implementation in later stages.

## Acceptable Reductions
Certain components of an SEO project can be scaled back without harming the
core investigative process.

Examples include:
- optimizing fewer pages during the initial phase
- limiting content refresh to priority articles
- focusing on the most valuable keyword clusters

Reducing volume rather than eliminating analysis helps maintain quality.

## Non‑Negotiable Components
Certain services should rarely be removed during negotiation.

Examples include:
technical audits
keyword research
diagnostic analysis

These activities identify the underlying cause of a client’s problem.
Without them, implementation work becomes guesswork.

## Budget Alignment Strategy
Sales teams should work with clients to align project scope with available
resources.

Common strategies include:
starting with a diagnostic-only engagement
phasing work across multiple months
prioritizing high‑impact improvements first

This structure preserves strategic integrity.

## Package Design
Reduced-scope packages should still follow a clear workflow.

1 investigation
2 analysis
3 targeted implementation

Skipping the investigation phase often leads to ineffective work.

## Client Communication
Clients should understand why certain tasks cannot be removed.

Explaining the relationship between diagnostic work and implementation
helps clients appreciate the value of the proposed engagement.

Clear explanations often prevent unrealistic scope reduction requests.

## Warning Signals
Sales teams should be cautious when clients insist on removing foundational
work entirely.

Such requests may indicate unrealistic expectations about the complexity of
SEO improvements.

Documenting these cases protects both the client and the agency.

## Outcome Principle
Effective scope reduction preserves investigation while reducing execution
volume. This approach ensures the engagement still produces actionable
insights and measurable improvements.
