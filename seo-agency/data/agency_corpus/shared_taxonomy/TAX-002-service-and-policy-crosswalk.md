# Service and Policy Crosswalk

## Purpose

The service and policy crosswalk connects three important
elements of the agency knowledge system.

client concerns  
available services  
relevant policies  

This crosswalk helps analysts navigate the knowledge base
and determine appropriate recommendations.

---

## Core Entities

The crosswalk links the following components.

concerns  
services  
policies  
playbooks  

These entities form the foundation of the agency’s
decision framework.

---

## Concern to Service Mapping

The first step in engagement planning is mapping
a client concern to possible services.

Examples include:

rankings_loss → technical SEO audit  
traffic_decline → content refresh  
indexing_issues → indexing remediation  
local_visibility_decline → local SEO foundation  
conversion_drop → conversion path analysis  

This mapping ensures the correct service category is evaluated.

---

## Service to Policy Mapping

Services may reference pricing or negotiation policies.

For example:

technical SEO audit → audit prerequisite policy  
keyword research → bundle pricing policy  
local SEO foundation → discount cap policy  

Policies ensure that service recommendations remain consistent
with agency guidelines.

---

## Service to Playbook Mapping

Playbooks provide operational guidance for addressing
specific types of concerns.

Examples include:

rankings loss playbook  
local visibility decline playbook  
technical incident response playbook  

These documents guide analysts during implementation.

---

## Decision Workflow

The crosswalk supports a structured decision process.

1 identify the client concern  
2 retrieve relevant services  
3 review applicable policies  
4 consult playbooks if needed  
5 propose engagement scope  

Following this workflow ensures consistency.

---

## Example Crosswalk

Client statement:

“Our local rankings disappeared from Google Maps.”

Concern:

local_visibility_decline

Possible services:

local SEO foundation  
Google Business Profile optimization

Relevant playbook:

local visibility decline playbook

---

## Retrieval Integration

The crosswalk document also supports knowledge retrieval.

Search systems can use the crosswalk to identify
related documents across different categories.

For example a query about rankings loss may retrieve:

service documents  
policy documents  
case studies  
playbooks

---

## Operational Benefit

Maintaining a crosswalk improves collaboration between
sales teams, analysts, and technical specialists.

Everyone operates using the same conceptual framework.

---

## Usage Guidance

Analysts should consult the crosswalk when uncertain
which services correspond to a particular concern.

This document acts as a navigation map for the
agency knowledge base.
