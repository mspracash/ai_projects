# Concern Taxonomy

## Purpose

The concern taxonomy provides a structured vocabulary for describing
SEO problems reported by clients.

Standardizing concern terminology allows analysts to categorize issues
consistently and retrieve the most relevant service documentation.

This taxonomy is used across the agency knowledge base.

---

## Core Entities

The taxonomy contains several primary concern categories.

rankings_loss  
traffic_decline  
indexing_issues  
local_visibility_decline  
conversion_drop  

Each concern category represents a common pattern observed during
SEO engagements.

---

## Concern Definitions

### Rankings Loss

Rankings loss refers to a decline in search engine positions for
previously ranking keywords.

Common causes include algorithm updates, content competition,
or technical site changes.

---

### Traffic Decline

Traffic decline occurs when organic search visits decrease
over a measurable time period.

This concern may originate from rankings loss, indexing issues,
or seasonal search patterns.

---

### Indexing Issues

Indexing issues occur when important pages fail to appear
in search engine indexes.

Typical causes include crawl errors, noindex directives,
or incorrect canonical tags.

---

### Local Visibility Decline

Local visibility decline refers to reduced presence in
map results or local search packs.

Businesses may experience this when competitors improve
their local listings or when citation consistency weakens.

---

### Conversion Drop

Conversion drop occurs when website traffic remains stable
but lead generation or purchases decline.

This often indicates usability problems or messaging issues.

---

## Service Associations

Each concern category typically maps to specific services.

For example:

rankings_loss → technical audit or keyword research  
traffic_decline → content refresh or technical investigation  
indexing_issues → indexing remediation  
local_visibility_decline → local SEO foundation  
conversion_drop → conversion path analysis  

These associations help guide service recommendations.

---

## Policy Relationships

Certain policies influence how services are recommended.

Pricing policies determine acceptable engagement structures.

Negotiation policies govern scope adjustments during sales discussions.

The taxonomy therefore links concerns not only to services
but also to related policies.

---

## Cross Mapping

The taxonomy acts as the first step in the agency’s diagnostic workflow.

1 identify concern  
2 retrieve relevant services  
3 reference related policies  
4 recommend an engagement plan  

This structured approach ensures consistent recommendations.

---

## Example Mapping

Client statement:

“Our rankings dropped after our website redesign.”

Mapped concern:

rankings_loss

Potential services:

technical SEO audit  
indexing remediation  

---

## Retrieval Notes

The taxonomy improves knowledge retrieval by providing
consistent vocabulary for indexing documentation.

Semantic retrieval systems can use these standardized
terms to locate relevant documents.

---

## Usage Guidance

Analysts should assign the most appropriate concern
category before recommending services.

Correct categorization improves diagnostic accuracy
and ensures consistent service proposals.
