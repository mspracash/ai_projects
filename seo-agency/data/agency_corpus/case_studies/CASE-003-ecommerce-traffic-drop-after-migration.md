# Case Study: Ecommerce Traffic Drop After Migration

## Client Snapshot
A mid‑size ecommerce retailer migrated its website to a new platform.
The migration was intended to improve page speed and site management.
Shortly after launch the company observed a sharp drop in organic traffic.

## Initial Symptoms
Organic sessions declined by nearly 40 percent.
Several top category pages disappeared from search results.
Search Console reported a large increase in crawl errors.

## Observed Evidence
URL structures had changed during the migration.
Many legacy URLs returned 404 errors.
Internal linking structures were partially broken.
Category pages also lost keyword‑rich content during redesign.

## Technical Evidence
A crawl analysis revealed more than 2,000 missing redirects.
Canonical tags were incorrectly implemented on many product pages.
Robots directives prevented some category pages from being indexed.
Search Console coverage reports confirmed indexation loss.

## Hypothesis
The migration introduced technical issues that blocked search engine access.
Broken redirects prevented link equity from transferring to new URLs.
Thin category content weakened relevance signals.

## Recommended Services
Technical SEO audit
Redirect mapping reconstruction
Category content refresh
Ongoing technical monitoring

## Execution
Analysts rebuilt redirect mappings from historical URLs.
Canonical tags were corrected across the site.
Category descriptions were rewritten with optimized keywords.
Internal links were restored between major category sections.

## Outcome
Within two months indexed pages increased significantly.
Organic traffic gradually recovered to pre‑migration levels.
Several high‑value category keywords returned to page one results.

## Lessons Learned
Website migrations carry significant SEO risk.
Detailed redirect planning is essential before launch.
Post‑migration audits should always be performed quickly.

## Transferable Pattern
Many ecommerce migrations unintentionally break indexing signals.
Careful testing before deployment can prevent most of these problems.
