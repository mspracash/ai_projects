# Organic Traffic Decline Playbook

## Trigger
This playbook is used when a client reports a measurable decline in organic website traffic.
Typical symptoms include fewer visits from search engines, declining impressions in Google Search Console, or reduced lead generation from organic channels.
Clients often detect this problem when marketing dashboards show a downward trend in monthly traffic.

## Immediate Checks
Confirm the decline using analytics platforms such as Google Analytics.
Compare organic traffic performance over the past three to six months.
Verify whether the decline is gradual or sudden.
Determine whether the traffic drop affects the entire website or only specific pages.

## Diagnostic Questions
Ask the client whether major website changes occurred recently.
Examples include site redesigns, content restructuring, or CMS migrations.
Determine whether new competitors have entered the market.
Ask whether recent marketing campaigns shifted focus away from organic search.

## Evidence Sources
Review Google Search Console impression and click data.
Analyze analytics reports for landing pages that lost traffic.
Compare traffic patterns before and after the decline began.
Evaluate keyword ranking reports for major position changes.

## Common Root Causes
Organic traffic declines often occur due to ranking losses.
Technical SEO problems may also reduce crawlability or indexing.
Content that becomes outdated or less relevant can lose visibility.
Competitors publishing stronger or more comprehensive content may also capture traffic.

## Recommended Services
Technical SEO audit.
Keyword research and opportunity analysis.
Content optimization and expansion.
Internal linking improvements.

## Execution Pattern
Identify pages with the largest traffic losses.
Analyze competing pages that now rank higher in search results.
Expand and refresh existing content to increase relevance.
Improve internal links pointing to affected landing pages.

## Escalation Path
If traffic continues declining after optimization, investigate deeper technical problems.
Review crawl errors, indexing status, and page performance.
Escalate complex technical problems to engineering teams.

## Example Signals
Gradual decrease in organic impressions.
Landing pages losing traffic despite stable rankings previously.
Competitors gaining traffic for important keywords.

## Resolution Pattern
Traffic recovery typically occurs after improving content quality and topical coverage.
Search engines reassess improved pages after recrawling.
Traffic growth usually returns gradually rather than immediately.

## Notes
Organic traffic trends fluctuate naturally over time.
However sustained declines typically signal structural SEO problems.
Continuous monitoring helps detect issues before they significantly impact revenue.
