# Organic Rankings Loss Playbook

## Trigger
This playbook is activated when a client reports a noticeable decline in organic search rankings.
Typical indicators include reduced keyword positions, falling impressions, or declining organic traffic.
Clients often notice ranking losses when previously high-performing keywords begin slipping to lower search positions.

## Immediate Checks
Verify the decline using reliable ranking data sources.
Review keyword tracking reports across the previous three to six months.
Confirm whether the decline affects a small group of keywords or entire sections of the site.
Check whether the decline aligns with a known search engine algorithm update.

## Diagnostic Questions
Ask the client whether recent changes occurred on the website.
Examples include redesigns, CMS migrations, or major content updates.
Confirm whether new competitors entered the market recently.
Determine whether new marketing campaigns changed site structure or navigation.

## Evidence Sources
Review Google Search Console for impression and ranking changes.
Examine analytics platforms for organic traffic patterns.
Use keyword tracking tools to measure ranking volatility.
Compare competitor rankings to identify emerging competitors.

## Common Root Causes
Rankings often drop after technical website changes.
Broken internal links and redirect issues may disrupt ranking signals.
Content updates that remove key topics can also reduce relevance.
Algorithm updates may reward competitor content with stronger topical authority.

## Recommended Services
Technical SEO audit.
Keyword opportunity analysis.
Content optimization and expansion.
Internal linking improvements.

## Execution Pattern
Identify pages losing the most rankings.
Analyze competing pages now ranking above the client.
Expand content depth and improve topical coverage.
Improve internal links pointing to affected pages.

## Escalation Path
If rankings continue declining after optimization, escalate the issue.
Investigate crawl errors, indexing issues, and algorithm penalties.
Coordinate with technical SEO specialists to identify deeper structural issues.

## Example Signals
Multiple keywords dropping several positions simultaneously.
Organic traffic decreasing across multiple landing pages.
Competitors suddenly outranking established pages.

## Resolution Pattern
Ranking recovery typically occurs after improving page relevance and internal linking.
Search engines gradually reassess improved pages.
Ranking recovery may take several weeks depending on crawl frequency.

## Notes
Organic rankings fluctuate naturally over time.
However sustained ranking loss usually indicates structural or competitive changes.
Regular monitoring helps detect early warning signals before traffic losses become severe.
